Use Cases

See Caddie in action.

Real scenarios CAD engineers face every day.

🔧
Debugging

Stop searching forums.

Your sketch shows dangling relations after renaming a reference plane. Instead of searching StackOverflow for an hour, ask Caddie — it sees your feature tree and tells you exactly which relation to delete and what to replace it with.

My sketch has a dangling relation after I renamed Top Plane
In Boss-Extrude1 sketch, the coincident relation on line 3 references the old plane name. Right-click it → Delete Relation → re-add Coincident to Top Plane.
🎓
Certification

Study with your model open.

Working toward CSWP certification? Caddie walks you through practice exercises step by step, referencing your actual model. Ask it to explain why a feature was built a certain way and get exam-level answers.

Walk me through this lofted feature for my CSWP prep
Your Loft1 uses 3 profiles and 1 guide curve — a common CSWP question type. The key constraint examiners test: guide curves must pierce all profiles, not just intersect.
🔍

How It Works

Up and running in under 5 minutes.

Caddie runs entirely inside your CAD software as a native add-in. No browser, no copy-paste, no context switching.

1

Install the add-in

Extract the zip, right-click install.bat → Run as Administrator, enable Caddie under Tools → Add-Ins. No account needed.

2

Open your model

Open any part, assembly, or drawing. Caddie automatically captures your feature tree, configuration, and a viewport screenshot.

3

Ask anything

Type your question. Caddie responds with specific advice referencing your actual feature names, sketches, and mates.
